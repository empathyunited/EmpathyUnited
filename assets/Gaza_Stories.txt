Abdullah-

My name is Abdullah. I am 29 years old, and I live in northern Gaza.

Since October 7, our lives have completely changed.

Before the war, we lived a simple but stable life. We had a home and small dreams that we worked toward every day. But our house was bombed and completely destroyed, and we lost our source of income. Since then, we have been relying on donations to meet our most basic needs.
Today, I am the sole provider for my family. I am doing everything I can to raise donations so I can feed my family and provide the minimum necessities for them.

We are now living in worn-out tents that do not protect us from the cold or the strong winds. Life has become a daily struggle just to survive. My father is ill and needs weekly medical treatment, but covering the cost of his care has become a major challenge under these conditions.

I survived death several times, but I lost my mother and my brother when our home was bombed. This pain will stay with me forever. Still, I try to remain strong for the sake of the family members who are still with me.

Despite everything, we hold onto hope. We believe that God will not abandon us, and that one day peace will return, and we will rebuild our lives.
If you are able to help us, your support — no matter how small — can make a real difference in our lives and help us provide food, medical care, and shelter for our family. We need your support now more than ever.

Thank you to everyone who listens to our story and stands with us during this difficult time 🥺💗


Ahmad-

Hello, my name is Ahmad. I am 22 years old from northern Gaza, specifically from Jabalia. I was studying Business Administration at Al-Azhar University, and I was excelling in my studies. We had a large supermarket, my mother was a teacher, and we had a beautiful and very big home.
Then suddenly, the war came. I completely lost my education after the university was bombed. We were displaced from our home to the south of the Gaza Strip, and the real and brutal war began. We lived in tents, and we are still living in them.
My father passed away during this war due to the deep sorrow caused by the complete destruction of our home and our supermarket, and because there was no access to proper medical treatment. After a long time, he was able to travel to Egypt with my mother and my sister Hala, and he passed away there. I have not seen my mother and my sister for an entire year because of the war and the closure of the crossings.
Then the terrible famine came. I lost 32 kilograms of my weight, and I lost friends while they were trying to receive humanitarian aid. The war ended, but just one week later, the bombing suddenly returned, and my lifelong friend, my childhood companion, and the person I loved most—my friend Abdullah—was martyred.
Now, every so often, the bombing resumes again. After the outbreak of the Iran war, aid to Gaza was closed once more, prices increased, and it seems that famine may return.
In conclusion, the Rafah crossing has been opened, and I am trying to raise money in order to escape this difficult reality, to see life again, continue my education, reunite with my mother whom I have been separated from, and visit my father’s grave in Egypt.
I hope you will look at me with compassion and mercy.



Omer Abu Amer-

Before October 7th, my life was beautiful.

I had a home that sheltered me, a loving family, friends who laughed with me, a job I loved, and a university I was proud of. I lived a normal life, full of details that gave me security and joy.

But nothing of that remains.

The occupation destroyed my home.

It took my family from me.

Many of my friends were killed.

My business was completely destroyed.

And my university… it's gone, bombed to the ground.

Everything has changed. The life I knew is gone.

Food was cheap and available; we ate what we liked, whenever we wanted.

Gaza was full of beautiful places; we would go there to spend a peaceful day.

I used to sit with my friends, laughing, playing, dreaming…
But now, everything is shattered.

I feel like I'm living in a deserted village, worthless, voiceless.
Death passes by me every day, taking those I love, stealing me from within. Our most basic rights are being taken away: food, water, education, and even hope.

We search for food with great difficulty, and if we find it, it's exorbitantly priced and may cost us our lives.

Yes, simply trying to obtain aid could end up in a sack… either carried away in it, or returned in it.

Our right to education has become an unattainable dream after our universities and schools were destroyed, and our teachers were killed.

We are living through days of indescribable cruelty.

Nothing you see in videos or news reports can make you feel what we are going through.

Even for water, we have to travel long distances to collect a few drops that can be drunk.

In Gaza… those who die are the ones who survived, and those who remain alive are the ones who are tortured and wish for death.
